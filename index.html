08 Actor Worksheet

Character Pipeline Activity

This activity invites you to trace how your character may have moved through the radicalization cycle — the back-and-forth process of normalization, acclimation, and dehumanization that shaped who they are at the top of the play. There are no right answers. Use the dramaturg's suggestion as a starting point, then trust your own instincts. Fill this out digitally or print it.

The Cycle — Visual Reference

Normalization

Extreme ideas, rhetoric, or behaviors are made to seem ordinary or inevitable. Repeated exposure to a fringe idea until it no longer registers as alarming — it becomes the wallpaper.

✦ Your framing How does normalization function in this play — which ideas, through what mechanisms, for whom?

Acclimation

Where normalization is passive exposure, acclimation is active adjustment. A character begins to orient their identity and relationships around the normalized idea. Not yet committed — but becoming comfortable.

✦ Your framing What does it look like when a character settles into a worldview? What do they gain, give up?

Dehumanization

The endpoint — and the fuel for the next cycle. Once a character has dehumanized a target (or themselves), that act makes the next round of normalization easier. It loops back; it doesn't end.

✦ Your framing How does dehumanization manifest in this play — in language, action, relationship?

The Loop

These stages feed each other recursively. Each cycle of normalization makes acclimation faster; each act of dehumanization becomes the new baseline to normalize. The pipeline is not a line — it's a spiral.

✦ Your framing How does this recursive quality show up in the structure of the play itself?
